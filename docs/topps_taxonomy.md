# Advanced Optical Recognition Taxonomy for Modern Topps Baseball Trading Cards

Before diving into the set-specific checklists, the computer vision pipeline
must be calibrated to distinguish between the two core substrates: **Flagship
Paper** and **Chrome**.

- **Flagship Paper Foils:** These apply metallic foil onto traditional
  cardboard. A **Rainbow Foil** features a smooth, continuous gradient of
  spectral colors shifting with light, lacking geometric patterns. A **Holo
  Foil** diffuses light differently, relying on a fine, glittering particulate
  shimmer. **Crackle Foil** looks like shattered glass or fractured ice with
  sharp intersecting boundaries, while **Diamante Foil** uses tightly packed,
  repeating faceted diamonds or triangles. **Sandglitter** reads as a
  high-density, crystalline sand or heavy grit texture.
- **Chrome Refractors:** Printed on thick, rigid poly-laminated chromium stock.
  An **X-Fractor** presents a strict, perpendicular checkerboard grid that
  alternates light and dark squares. A **Wave** refractor uses parallel,
  oscillating wavy lines, whereas a **RayWave** refractor features chaotic,
  squiggly lines radiating outward from a focal point. A **Prism** features
  shattered diamond-like shards on thick stock, and a **Lava** refractor mimics
  a fluid, melted metallic blob pattern.
